Projects

On this page, you find a quick overview of my current and recent research projects. Most of these have Web demonstrators or resources available.

Multilingual Semantic Role Labeling

In this line of research, I am building a semantic parser capable of parsing a wide range of languages such as Arabic, Chinese, German, Hindi, Russian and many others. Next to building the parser, I am also working on semi-automatically generating appropriate training data from parallel corpora. We use an annotation projection approach to generate Proposition Banks for arbitrary languages. We are making all resources publicly available, so check out the project overview page for more details and the generated Proposition Banks.

Exploratory Information Extraction

In this line of research, I am investigating unsupervised and semi-supervised approach for Information Discovery and Exploratory Information Extraction in very large collections of unstructured data.

The Weltmodell - A Data-Driven Commonsense Knowledge Base

In this line of work, we used OpenIE and distributional similarity on an extremely large corpus provided by Google to create a knowledge base of commonsense statements. I am currently working on using this knowledge to handle NLP tasks.

Resources:
  • Demo: Browse the Weltmodell online here!
  • Data: You can download the Weltmodell data. If you like the data, please contact me and let me know what you're using it for!
  • Paper: The knowledge base is described in our LREC 2014 paper [pdf].

Nerdle: Topic-Specific Question Answering

This is my student Umar Maqsud's work (who, incidentally, now also works at IBM). The idea here was to use OpenIE techniques on large collections of topically focused text. In our case, we used the topics "Star Trek", "Star Wars" and "The Simpsons", and used a focused crawler to retrieve as much text as possible from the Web. The goal was to investigate in how far OpenIe can be used as basis for question-answering systems.

Resources:
  • Demo: Ask Nerdle some questions online here!
  • Paper: The knowledge base is described in our COLING 2014 demo paper [pdf].
