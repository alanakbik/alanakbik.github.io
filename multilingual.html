Multilingual Semantic Role Labeling

In this line of research, our goal is to enable shallow semantic parsing of arbitrary languages into one unified semantic abstraction.

Polyglot

I am building a system (called Polyglot) that is currently capable of semantically parsing 9 different languages. Check out the three screenshots below for example sentences in English, Mandarin Chinese and Spanish. No matter what input language, the system identifies the same semantic concepts such as buyer, thing bought, price paid or temporal context.



This unified semantic abstraction may serve as basis for applications such as crosslingual information extraction or question answering. In fact, one of my active fields of research is to utilize this system for multilingual data mining applications at IBM.

We will demo the system at ACL 2016. The corresponding publication is:

Polyglot: Multilingual Semantic Role Labeling with Unified Labels. Alan Akbik and Yunyao Li. 54th Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics, ACL 2016. [pdf]

Generating Multilingual Proposition Banks

We require Proposition Bank-style resources for arbitrary languages in order to train our system. Unfortunately, such corpora generally do not exist for most languages and are prohibitively expensive to manually produce.

For this reason I am investigating semi-automatic approaches of creating Proposition Banks for arbitrary languages.

In its first incarnation, our approach is fully automatic and based on annotation projection in parallel corpora. We specifically address problems of translation shifts with a two step approach: In the first step, projectable labels are identified (see Figure) and projected. In the second step, a bootstrapping approach repeatedly samples the target language corpus to estimate labels for all unlabeled target language consitutents.

Example of annotation projection for one sentence pair. English labels can in some cases (blue) be projected onto French words.

Using this approach, we auto-generated Proposition Banks for 7 languages. The corresponding publication is:

Generating High Quality Proposition Banks for Multilingual Semantic Role Labeling. Alan Akbik, Laura Chiticariu, Marina Danilevsky, Yunyao Li, Shivakumar Vaithyanathan and Huaiyu Zhu. 53rd Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics, ACL 2015. [pdf]
