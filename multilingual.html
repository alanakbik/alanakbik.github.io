Multilingual Text Analytics

This page gives an overview of my work on multilingual text data. My goal is twofold: 1. To enable shallow semantic parsing of arbitrary languages into one unified semantic abstraction. 2. To enable multilingual text analytics over this shared abstraction.

Multilingual Semantic Parsing

I am building a system (called Polyglot ) that is currently capable of semantically parsing 9 different languages. Check out the three screenshots below for example sentences in English, Mandarin Chinese and Spanish.



No matter what input language, the system identifies the same semantic concepts such asbuyer,thing bought, price paid or temporal context. These unified semantic labels enable text analytics that automatically works across a range of different languages.

We demo'ed the system at ACL 2016. The corresponding publication is:

Polyglot: Multilingual Semantic Role Labeling with Unified Labels. Alan Akbik and Yunyao Li. 54th Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics, ACL 2016. [pdf]

Crosslingual Information Extraction

We are using this unified semantic abstraction as basis for applications such as crosslingual information extraction or question answering.

For instance, the this screen shows the output of an extractor capable of detecting a Buyer-ThingBought relation in multilingual text. It was created only once for one language - but automatically works for a large number of supported languages without any additional effort!

Auto-Generating Proposition Banks

We require labeled data to train our semantic parser. Unfortunately, such corpora generally do not exist for most languages and are prohibitively expensive to manually produce.

For this reason I am investigating semi-automatic approaches of creating Proposition Banks for arbitrary languages. We require only parallel corpora as input and limited human interaction to create high-quality resources.

Using this approach, we auto-generated Proposition Banks for 7 languages. The corresponding publication is:

Example of annotation projection for one sentence pair.

Generating High Quality Proposition Banks for Multilingual Semantic Role Labeling. Alan Akbik, Laura Chiticariu, Marina Danilevsky, Yunyao Li, Shivakumar Vaithyanathan and Huaiyu Zhu. 53rd Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics, ACL 2015. [pdf]

Low-Resource Languages

Projecting syntactic and semantic labels from English to Tamil.

We are also interested in text analytics and semantic parsing over low-resource languages.

We applied our approach to Bengali, Malayalam and Tamil, three low-resource languages with over 300 million first language speakers - but almost no NLP resources and tools.

We propose a combination of annotation projection, crowdsourcing and limited expert involvement to create NLP resources for low-resource languages in a cost-effective way. The corresponding publication is:

Towards Semi-Automatic Generation of Proposition Banks for Low-Resource Languages. Alan Akbik and Yunyao Li. 2016 Conference on Empirical Methods on Natural Language Processing, EMNLP 2016.
