Multilingual Semantic Role Labeling

In this line of research, our goal is to enable shallow semantic parsing of arbitrary languages. One crucial bottleneck is the availability of suitable training data for shallow semantic parsers: While for English such data exists in the form of text data labeled with Propbank labels, such corpora generally do not exist for most other languages and are very expensive to produce.

We investigated a method for automatically generating such data, using annotation projection in parallel corpora. Our approach is designed to sidestep problems stemming from translation shifts. It works in two steps: In the first step, projectable labels are identified (see Figure) and projected. In the second step, a bootstrapping approach repeatedly samples the target language corpus to estimate labels for all unlabeled target language consitutents.

Please refer to (Akbik et al., 2015) for a detailed overview of the approach and the results.

Example of annotation projection for one sentence pair. The English labels can in some cases be projected onto French words. Our approach determines projectable labels (blue) and blocks projection of all other labels (red).
